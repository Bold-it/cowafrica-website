OUR HISTORY

The Center for One Welfare – Africa (COW-Africa) was established by Richard Klottey Botchway, a Veterinary Paraprofessional (Veterinary Technician) focused solely on animal health concerns as RicKELL Animal Care Services (RACS), a profitable animal health service provider for pet owners and livestock farmers in Ghana, starting in September 2011, mainly serving cities and peri-urban communities. In April 2014, RACS rebranded as RicKELL Animal Health Consult & Services (RAHCS), with expanded expertise and a broader coverage area.

In March 2018, due to concerns and outbreaks of Hepatitis B, Ebola Virus Disease, and Lassa Fever in neighboring countries, RAHCS began public health education in rural communities, religious gatherings, and other places. This initiative was led by Fredricka Nayram Affram, a Nursing Assistant Clinicals (NAC). Realizing that most rural community dwellers could not afford the costs of vaccinations, deworming, and treatments for their backyard farm livestock, which provide their livelihood, RAHCS was transformed into a Non-Profit organization called RicKELL Animal Welfare – Ghana (RAW-GH). This led to an increase in the reach of the health campaigns. More health screenings, deworming, and animal healthcare were carried out through Free-Mass, Subsidized, or Fully Paid services (for the wealthy), funded by sponsorships, grants, and donations from religious groups (during crusades and evangelism events), community leaders, industry players in the health sector, corporate organizations, and others.

Drawing inspiration from the One Health concept, the idea of creating an umbrella organization to address human and animal health issues came to fruition in 2020 with the establishment of the Center for One Welfare (COW) in Ghana. By networking with professionals across Africa, COW's activities and operations have expanded beyond Ghana's borders. To create a unified body and to enhance our reach and impact, the Center for One Welfare was fully incorporated in February 2025 as the CENTER FOR ONE WELFARE – AFRICA, abbreviated as COW-Africa, with the slogan, ‘Uniting for a Healthier World’, which reflects our aim to garner effort to improve the health and well-being of all species. We are committed to optimizing multistage and multisectoral collaborative actions that integrate human, animal, and environmental health, striving for a healthier and more resilient future for all.
